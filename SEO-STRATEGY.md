# SEO and Local Growth Strategy

## What this pack is designed to do
1. Improve local relevance for Lexington, Thomasville, Salisbury, High Point, and Davidson County.
2. Build topical depth around managed IT, Microsoft 365, cybersecurity, backup, migration, and compliance support.
3. Create internal linking opportunities between services, verticals, locations, and blog content.
4. Make the site more useful for conversion by keeping contact information and requests for information visible.

## Recommended next steps after launch
- Create and optimize the Google Business Profile for Lazy Dog Computing.
- Add real business photos, office photos, or local imagery over time.
- Ask for reviews consistently after successful work.
- Publish one to two new articles per week after the initial thirty-day batch.
- Add backlinks from the Chamber of Commerce, local directories, partners, and vendor pages.
- Add service testimonials and case studies as they become available.

## Internal linking plan
- Link the home page to Services, Contact, and the four vertical pages.
- Link each blog article to one related service page and the contact page.
- Link service pages to relevant vertical and location pages where natural.
- Keep the contact page linked from the main navigation and in calls to action.

## Conversion tips
- Keep the phone number visible.
- Add a hosted form processor after deployment.
- Add real testimonials and logos once available.
- Add a clear free assessment offer if desired.

## Honest expectation
The site is built for strong local relevance and content coverage, but ranking improvements depend on execution after launch. Reviews, backlinks, and Google Business Profile activity will matter a lot.
